How to educate prospects at educational institutions — 6-step plan
Step 1 — Problem-first cold outreach Week 1–2
Don't lead with product. Intel agent identifies each institution's pain: overcrowded labs, outdated library, hostel renovation tender, new campus phase. Outreach agent personalises message around that specific problem. Subject line: "How [Institution Name] can modernise its [specific space]".
Step 2 — Send a "Space Insight Report" Week 2–3
A one-page PDF or microsite tailored to the institution type (e.g. "Designing the Modern Engineering Campus"). Show peer institutions globally that used KI — case studies from IITs, US universities, etc. Include KI's Classroom Planner tool and link to it. This is not a brochure — it is genuinely educational content.
Step 3 — Virtual "Space Design Workshop" invite Week 3–4
Host a 45-min online session: "Future-Ready Campus Design for Bangalore Institutions". Invite facilities heads, principals, and admin officers. Cover active learning research, space flexibility, sustainability (KI's LEED-certified products), and show real-time layouts using KI's Classroom Planner. No hard sell — pure education with KI as the expert host.
Step 4 — On-site "Space Audit" offer Week 4–6
Offer a free, no-obligation campus walk-through and space audit. Bring physical samples (fabric swatches, material samples, product brochures). Create a personalised floor-plan proposal using KI's Classroom Planner. This converts the prospect from educated to engaged. Tie to NAAC accreditation improvement or NEP 2020 compliance where relevant.
Step 5 — ROI Presentation to decision makers Week 6–8
Present to the finance/admin committee. Frame in terms of: student capacity per sq. ft., furniture lifespan vs local alternatives, warranty value, resale consideration. Highlight KI's employee-owned model as a quality assurance signal. Show total cost of ownership comparison vs cheaper alternatives.
Step 6 — Pilot order + campus showcase Week 8–12
Propose a pilot: furnish one room or space at a preferential rate. Photograph it. Let it become a showroom. Other institutions visiting for events see KI furniture in use on a Bangalore campus — peer validation is the most powerful closer. Outreach agent follows up with photos and offer to peer institutions.

12-week execution plan
Week 1–2: Scout & Build
Scout agent maps 200+ Bangalore institutions. Intel agent enriches top 50 with decision-maker names, budget signals, renovation news. CEO agent approves first target batch. Build Space Insight Report template.
Week 3–4: First outreach wave
Outreach agent sends personalised Email 1 + 2 to all 50 targets. Track open rates. Intel agent monitors LinkedIn for response signals. Scout agent continues to build Tier 2 list.
Week 5–6: Workshop & follow-up
Host virtual Space Design Workshop. Send Email 3 (workshop invite) to all leads. Warm leads (opened 2+ emails, clicked) receive audit offer (Email 4). CEO agent reviews pipeline, re-prioritises.
Week 7–8: On-site audits
Book and execute campus walk-throughs for top 10 engaged leads. Deliver personalised layout proposals using KI Classroom Planner. Schedule ROI presentations with decision-maker committees.
Week 9–10: ROI presentations
Present to finance/admin committees at top 5 prospects. Address procurement process — GEM portal registration, tender documentation, CSR budget alignment. Propose pilot room order.
Week 11–12: Close pilots, expand list
Close 2–3 pilot orders. Photograph installations. Outreach agent uses photos as social proof in next wave. Scout agent expands to 100 new targets. CEO agent reviews cost-per-lead and optimises agent weights for next quarter.
