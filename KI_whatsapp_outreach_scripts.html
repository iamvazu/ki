WhatsApp outreach scripts — college admin heads
KI Imago furniture · Bangalore institutions · Parallel to email sequence
Before sending anything: WhatsApp outreach to unknown numbers without prior contact is legally grey under TRAI regulations and practically risky — an early "Block" ends all future contact. These scripts assume either (a) the number was shared voluntarily (enquiry form, event, referral), (b) you've already exchanged at least one email, or (c) you have a warm mutual introduction. Never send these cold to scraped numbers.
1
First touch — warm intro message
Day 1
After email sent
You send
Hello [Name] sir/ma'am, This is [Your Name] from KI Imago — we manufacture school and college furniture here in Bengaluru (Jigni). I sent an email to your institution's official address earlier today regarding classroom and library furniture. Wanted to make sure it reached you directly. If you have 2 minutes at a convenient time, I'd love to share something relevant to [College Name]'s infrastructure. No obligation at all. Thank you for your time. — [Your Name], KI Imago
Why this works: References the email so you're not appearing from nowhere. "2 minutes" is a low-commitment ask. Mentioning Bengaluru/Jigni immediately signals local manufacturer — not a random pan-India spam message. Keep it under 5 lines. Do not send images, PDFs, or links in the first message — WhatsApp spam filters and human instinct both flag these.
2
Follow-up if no reply in 3 days
Day 4
No reply to message 1
You send
Hi [Name] sir/ma'am, Quick follow-up — wanted to share one specific thing rather than send a long message. We recently furnished the [library/classroom block — use whichever is most relevant] at a Bangalore college similar to [College Name]. The admin team found our 2-4 week local delivery particularly useful when working against a semester deadline. If furniture is something [College Name] is thinking about — even for next year — happy to send across 2-3 photos and a rough range of what it costs. No formal proposal, just context. Would that be useful?
The ask is tiny: "Would that be useful?" requires a yes/no answer — the smallest possible commitment. Mentioning delivery timeline before price is intentional: it positions you as solving a logistics problem, not just selling furniture. Send between 10 AM–12 PM on a weekday — avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons.

Send images separately, not in a single burst. Two images with text captions, sent 30–60 seconds apart, feel like a conversation — not a catalogue dump. The final question ("what space is most pressing?") is the most important line in this exchange. The answer tells you exactly what to pitch next and what to bring to the campus visit.
4
Booking the campus visit
Day 7–10
After they engage with photos
You send
Hi [Name] sir/ma'am, Thank you for the feedback on the photos. We usually find the most useful next step is a quick visit — we come to [College Name], walk the space with you or your team, and tell you honestly what would work and what wouldn't. Takes about 45 minutes. No presentation, no slides. Just a walk and a conversation. We're available any morning this week or next — whichever is convenient. Would [Tuesday morning] or [Thursday morning] work?
Give two specific options, not "any time that suits you." Open-ended scheduling invitations get deferred indefinitely. Two specific days force a decision. "No presentation, no slides" removes the admin head's biggest fear — that this is a 2-hour sales ordeal. If they suggest a different time, accept immediately without negotiating.

Two things this message does: (1) Signals professionalism — you're bringing samples, not just showing up. (2) Asks if the HOD or purchase officer can join — if they say yes, you've just expanded your access to the decision-making committee in a single WhatsApp message. This is one of the highest-leverage lines in the entire sequence.

Post-visit follow-up
Visit day +1
Next morning after the visit
You send — next morning
Good morning [Name] sir/ma'am, Thank you for the time yesterday — it was genuinely useful to see the [library/classroom block/seminar rooms] in person. As discussed, I'll prepare a layout proposal for [specific space they mentioned] and send it across by [day — give 3 business days maximum]. A few things I'll include: • Recommended furniture for each zone • Indicative quantities and pricing • Delivery timeline from our Jigni factory • 5-year warranty terms If there's anything specific you'd like me to address — budget range, preference on materials, a particular configuration — just let me know and I'll factor it in. Thank you again. Speak soon.
Speed is everything here. Send this within 18 hours of the visit — not 3 days later. The layout proposal must follow within 3 business days as promised. Admin heads talk to each other; a vendor who delivers on a commitment stands out sharply against those who say "I'll send it" and disappear for 2 weeks. The bullet list sets exact expectations so there are no surprises in the proposal.

Ghost recovery rules: Never send more than one ghost recovery message. If this gets no reply, move the contact to the "next-term nurture" list and reactivate in 8–10 weeks with a fresh hook — ideally tied to a new trigger (NAAC news, semester start, renovation announcement). The mention of a nearby installation is the most powerful re-engagement tool available.

Handle these on WhatsApp — fast, human, no panic
"We already have a vendor"
Do not argue. Acknowledge and plant a seed for when the relationship cracks — which it always eventually does.
Understood sir, no problem at all. If you ever need a second quote or your current vendor has a lead time issue, we're right here in Bengaluru — happy to step in. I'll check back in next term.
"Send a quote / price list"
Don't send a full price list — it anchors them to numbers before they understand value. Offer a visit-based quote instead.
Happy to sir. For an accurate quote I'd need to know the quantities and space layout — a 45-minute visit lets me give you a precise number rather than a generic range. Alternatively I can share our starting prices for the most common items if that helps for now?
"Budget is approved only for next year"
This is not a rejection — it's a timeline. Lock in the relationship now so you're their first call when the budget opens.
Perfectly fine sir — our best relationships start well before the budget is finalised. If you'd like we can do the walk-through now so the proposal is ready the moment the PO process opens. No pressure otherwise — I'll follow up closer to your budget cycle.
"Is this on GeM?"
Critical question for government-aided institutions. Answer immediately and specifically — do not hedge.
Yes sir, we are [registered / in the process of registering] on GeM under furniture categories. Our GST is 29AAECK0473E1ZN and we can provide all documentation needed for your purchase order process. Shall I share the GeM seller details?
"Are you a local company or imported?"
This is your strongest card. Lead with manufacturing, end with heritage.
We manufacture right here in Bengaluru sir — our factory is in Bommasandra-Jigni, about 20 km from your campus. So delivery is 2–4 weeks and our after-sales team is local. We're part of the Krueger International family — a global institutional furniture brand — but everything for India is made here.
"Not interested"
Respect it completely. A graceful exit leaves the door open — a pushy response shuts it permanently.
Of course sir, I completely understand. Thank you for your time. If things change at [College Name] or you need a second opinion on a furniture decision, we're always available. Best wishes for the semester.
WhatsApp outreach — what works, what kills the conversation
Send between 9–11 AM or 2–4 PM on weekdays. Admin heads check WhatsApp during transit and between meetings. Monday morning and Friday after 3 PM are dead zones.
First message: text only. No links, no PDFs, no images. Anything that looks like a broadcast gets blocked or ignored. The first message must feel personal.
Keep messages under 5 lines. If it requires scrolling, it won't be read. WhatsApp is not email — every message should fit in one screen without expanding.
Use "sir" or "ma'am" throughout. College admin heads in Bangalore expect formal address. Dropping it signals unfamiliarity with the culture and immediately flags you as an outsider.
Always end with a single, specific question. "Would Tuesday morning work?" closes better than "let me know your availability." One question, one answer required.
Never send a catalogue or price list as the second message. It signals you're a commodity vendor. Send a photo of a real installation with a caption — that signals you're a partner.
Never follow up more than twice without a response. Two unanswered messages is the absolute maximum. A third marks you as spam in their mind regardless of what it says.
Never use WhatsApp Business broadcast lists for these scripts. College admin heads can tell when a message was broadcast. The moment they sense it isn't personal, they disengage — even if the content is relevant.
Do not send the layout proposal or any document over WhatsApp. Send a link or a brief summary on WhatsApp, then email the full document. WhatsApp is for conversation — email is for documentation that needs to be forwarded to a committee.
Set up WhatsApp Business with a proper profile. Logo, company name (KI Imago), address (Jigni, Bengaluru), website, and catalogue. An admin head who gets your message and taps your profile should see a credible company — not a blank personal account.
Use voice notes sparingly, only after rapport is established. A 30-second voice note from someone they already know feels personal. From a stranger it feels intrusive. Never open with a voice note.
