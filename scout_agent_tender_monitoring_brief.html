Scout Agent — Tender Monitoring Brief
KI Imago · Bangalore educational institution furniture tenders · Daily operating manual
Priority order: GeM is checked first and most often — it is where government-aided colleges must buy from. KPPP is second for Karnataka state-funded institutions. CPPP covers central government bodies (IISc, ISI, ISRO). Individual institution portals catch what the aggregators miss.

GeM — Government e-Marketplace Check daily
mkp.gem.gov.in → Search → Custom Bids & Reverse Auction
What it covers
All central & state govt ministries, autonomous bodies, PSUs. Includes govt-aided colleges like UVCE, BIT, BMCRI, ISI, ISRO training centres. Mandatory for these institutions by Ministry of Finance order.
Search terms
school furniture educational furniture classroom furniture library furniture student chair student desk lab furniture college furniture seminar furniture cafeteria furniture 9401 9403
Filter by
State: Karnataka → Category: Furniture → Status: Live bids only → Sort by: Closing date ascending
Buyer types to watch
Universities, Autonomous Bodies, Educational Institutions, Research Organisations, Central PSUs in Bangalore
GeM bid modes
Direct Purchase (under ₹25K/item) — no bid needed, just list. L1 Purchase (₹25K–₹5L) — your price must be lowest. Bidding/Reverse Auction (above ₹5L) — monitor these daily. Custom Bids — institution specifies exact requirements; most relevant for large orders.
Alert setup
Create a GeM Seller account → go to "Bid Alerts" → set keyword + category alerts. GeM emails you when a matching bid is published. Do not rely on this alone — do manual checks too.

KPPP — Karnataka Public Procurement Portal Check daily
eproc.karnataka.gov.in → Live Tenders → Education
What it covers
Karnataka state government departments, state universities (Bangalore University, VTU), state-funded autonomous colleges, BBMP, KUIDFC, state PSUs. Covers VTU-affiliated government engineering colleges.
Search terms
furniture chairs tables desks benches classroom library college vidyalaya college furniture
Department filter
Higher Education Dept, Department of Public Instruction, Technical Education Dept, VTU, Bangalore University, Individual colleges
Registration needed
Yes — register as a bidder at eproc.karnataka.gov.in with DSC (Digital Signature Certificate). One-time activity. Required before you can download tender documents or submit bids.
Aggregator shortcut
Use bidassist.com or tendersplus.com to get KPPP alerts by keyword without logging into the portal daily — saves ~30 min/day.

Individual institution tender pages Check weekly
College websites → Procurement / Tenders / Notice Board section
Why this matters
Private deemed universities (Christ, PES, Jain, Reva) are NOT on GeM or KPPP. They publish tenders only on their own websites or sometimes only on physical notice boards. These tenders are invisible to aggregators and have far less competition.
Priority pages to bookmark
Christ University → Administration → Tenders | PES University → About → Procurement | Jain University → Administration | MS Ramaiah Institute → Administration → Tenders | RVCE → Administration | BMS College → Principal's Office
What to look for
Pages titled: Tenders, Quotations, Procurement Notices, NIT (Notice Inviting Tender), Limited Tender. Also check PDF notice boards — many colleges scan and upload physical notices as PDFs.

Speed is the constraint. Most tenders have 7–21 day windows from publication to closing. The Scout Agent must qualify a tender and escalate within 24 hours of discovery — not within a week.
The 6-question qualification test — answer all 6 before escalating
1
Is the buyer a Bangalore educational institution or research body?
Check the organisation name and address in the tender header. Must be in Bengaluru or Bengaluru Rural district. Schools, colleges, universities, research institutes, and their affiliated training centres all qualify. Pure government department offices (PWD, BBMP roads) do not — even if they're furnishing an office.
2
Is the product category within KI Imago's manufacturing range?
KI Imago makes: student chairs, writing pad chairs, classroom desks, student school desks, cafeteria furniture, seminar tables, folding tables, library furniture, office workstations, waiting chairs. KI Imago does NOT make: construction materials, IT equipment, cleaning supplies, stationery, curtains, air conditioning, flooring. If the tender is for multiple categories bundled together (furniture + IT + flooring), check if you can bid for the furniture lot only.
3
Is the estimated value above ₹2 lakh?
Below ₹2 lakh: the margin after bid preparation cost and delivery rarely justifies the effort unless it's a relationship-building pilot. ₹2–10 lakh: moderate value, worth bidding if the institution is on the target list. Above ₹10 lakh: high priority — escalate immediately to CEO Agent. Above ₹50 lakh: flag as strategic — CEO Agent must be informed within 6 hours of discovery.
4
Is the closing date more than 5 working days away?
Less than 5 working days remaining: flag as urgent — bid preparation may not be feasible. Escalate anyway so CEO Agent can decide whether to attempt a fast-track bid or skip. More than 5 days: normal processing. More than 14 days: comfortable window — schedule bid preparation in the week's plan rather than treating as urgent.
5
Are KI Imago's eligibility credentials likely to qualify?
Download the tender document and check Section 3 (Eligibility Criteria). Common requirements: minimum annual turnover (KI Imago: ₹25–100 Cr — confirm exact figure), years in business (incorporated 2010 — 14+ years), GST registration (confirmed: 29AAECK0473E1ZN), PAN (confirm), experience certificates (prior supply orders to educational institutions), IS/BIS certification (confirm which products are certified). If any eligibility criterion is clearly not met — flag as INELIGIBLE and move on. Do not attempt ineligible bids.
6
Is the technical specification compatible with KI Imago products?
Read the BoQ (Bill of Quantities) and technical specifications in the tender. Check: dimensions (seat height, table height, weight capacity), materials (polypropylene, leatherite, metal frame), finish (powder-coated, chrome), certifications required (IS:1303 for chairs, IS:4535 for tables — confirm with factory). If the spec is written for a specific competitor's product (very specific dimensions, brand-like requirements), note this as a "restrictive spec" and flag — you may still win if you can demonstrate equivalence, but the risk is higher.

Standard bid document checklist — what the factory and finance team must provide
1
Technical compliance statement
For each item in the BoQ: confirm your product model number, dimensions, material, weight capacity, and IS/BIS certification. If a spec deviation exists (e.g. slightly different dimension), state it explicitly with justification — do not hide deviations. Tender committees reject bids with undisclosed deviations after the fact.
2
Financial documents
GST registration certificate, PAN, audited financials for last 2–3 years (to prove turnover), bank solvency certificate (some tenders require this), EMD (Earnest Money Deposit) — typically 2% of bid value via DD or bank guarantee.
3
Experience certificates
Supply order copies or completion certificates from at least 3 prior institutional clients. If limited India institutional references are available, include manufacturing tenure, international KI brand association, and factory capability documents. Build this evidence file proactively — don't scramble for it when a tender appears.
4
Pricing and BoQ
Unit price per item inclusive of GST, delivery to site, and installation. Always include a "Make in India" declaration if products are locally manufactured — this activates the 20% purchase preference in government tenders. State delivery lead time: "2–4 weeks from PO date, from Bengaluru manufacturing facility."
5
Warranty and after-sales terms
State warranty period (5 years), what is covered (manufacturing defects, frame failure, surface peeling), response time for service calls (48 hours — local Bengaluru factory), and spare parts availability. This is a scored criterion in many education tenders — not just a formality.
The spec-in strategy — how to influence a tender before it's published
What spec-in means
When the Intel Agent's relationship work results in a campus visit or audit, the HOD or Admin Officer who writes the purchase indent often asks "what should we specify?" — this is your chance to suggest dimensions, materials, and quality benchmarks that your products naturally meet and competitors may not. A tender written with your specs is effectively pre-won before it opens.
What to suggest (without naming your brand)
Chair height: 18.5" seat height. Frame: 18-gauge powder-coated steel tube. Seat: high-density polypropylene, minimum 4mm thickness. Warranty: minimum 5 years. Certification: IS:1303 compliance required. Origin: Manufactured within Karnataka preferred (triggers local preference). These are your product specs — stated as quality requirements, not brand requirements.
The legal boundary
Specification suggestions must be based on genuine quality and safety rationale — not purely to exclude competitors. Suggesting IS:1303 certification is legitimate quality assurance. Suggesting a very specific proprietary dimension that only your exact product meets is restrictive and can invalidate the tender if challenged. Stay on the right side of this line.

When the Scout Agent escalates immediately — no waiting
Tender above ₹50 lakh, closing within 14 days
This is the highest-value scenario. Alert CEO Agent within 6 hours. Include: tender ID, portal link, BoQ download link, estimated value, closing date, and your 6-question qualification assessment. Do not wait for the daily report.
Any tender from IISc, IIM-B, NLSIU, or Azim Premji University
These are A+ priority institutions. Even a small tender from them — a pilot order for one seminar room — is strategically valuable as a reference installation. Escalate regardless of value.
A tender closing in less than 5 working days, any value above ₹5 lakh
Fast-closing tenders require immediate decision. CEO Agent needs to decide within 2 hours: attempt fast-track bid or skip. Give them all qualification data immediately so they can decide without further research.
A tender with a spec that looks written for a competitor
Flag as "restrictive spec — review needed." Include the specific dimension or requirement that seems non-standard. CEO Agent decides whether to bid with a deviation note or use the opportunity to build a relationship with the institution for the next cycle.
A corrigendum (amendment) to a tender already in bid prep
If specs, quantities, or closing dates change after you've started bid preparation, escalate immediately. Corrigenda can make a bid ineligible or change the pricing model entirely. Never submit against an old spec after a corrigendum is published.
A tender from an institution currently in active outreach relationship
This is the ideal convergence — Intel Agent and Outreach Agent have been building a relationship, and now a formal tender appears. Flag to CEO Agent and coordinate with Outreach Agent immediately. The relationship contact may share insights on committee preferences, budget constraints, or competing bids — this is legal and common in Indian procurement culture.
