Step 01

Receiving an escalation from Scout Agent

The Scout Agent sends an escalation message via WhatsApp (and logs it in the Tender Register). The CEO Agent's first job is to acknowledge, read, and triage within the SLA window — not to start preparing the bid.

SLA clock starts the moment the Scout Agent's message is sent — not when the CEO Agent reads it. Missing an SLA is a process failure. If the CEO Agent is unavailable, escalation should be forwarded to a designated backup immediately.
Tender Value Acknowledge SLA Bid Decision SLA Who decides
Above ₹50 lakh Within 2 hours Within 6 hours CEO Agent + Director / Owner
₹10 lakh – ₹50 lakh Within 4 hours Within 24 hours CEO Agent
₹2 lakh – ₹10 lakh Same business day Within 48 hours CEO Agent
Below ₹2 lakh Weekly batch review Weekly batch Admin / CEO Agent lightweight review

When an escalation is received, the CEO Agent immediately does three things before anything else: reads the Scout's qualification assessment in full, downloads the tender document if not already attached, and checks the Tender Register to confirm the row has been logged.

Step 02

The bid / no-bid decision

The Scout Agent has already done the 6-question qualification check. The CEO Agent's decision adds three further filters that the Scout cannot assess — capacity, strategic fit, and pricing viability.

1
Is the Scout's qualification assessment complete and correct?
Skim the tender document to verify the Scout's 6-point assessment. Pay particular attention to eligibility criteria and technical specs — these are where errors most often occur. If the qualification is wrong (Scout passed a tender that should have failed), mark as SKIP, update the Tender Register, and send the Scout Agent feedback on why.
2
Does the factory have capacity to fulfil within the tender's delivery window?
Call or message the factory directly. Ask: what is the current order backlog, and can we deliver [quantity from BoQ] within [delivery window in tender]? If the factory says no — do not bid. Winning a tender you cannot deliver is worse than not bidding. See Section 03 for the capacity check protocol.
3
Can we price competitively while maintaining margin?
Quick margin check: calculate the estimated cost to produce and deliver the BoQ quantity (factory cost + delivery + installation + EMD opportunity cost). If the margin at a competitive price is below 12%, flag for discussion before proceeding. Government tenders are L1 — someone will always go lower. Do not win at a loss to build a reference.
4
Is this a strategic relationship tender?
Check: is this institution in the active outreach pipeline? Has the Outreach Agent already met the Registrar or HOD? If yes — this tender is strategic regardless of value. A ₹3 lakh tender at IISc is worth bidding at thin margin because the reference is priceless. Flag this context to the factory and finance team when requesting pricing.
5
Make the decision and update the Tender Register
BID: update status to "Bid Prep", assign to Bid Tracker sheet, notify factory and finance. NO BID: update status to "Skipped", record reason in Notes column. This data is valuable — over time, skip reasons reveal patterns (always losing on price, always missing EMD, always spec mismatch) that inform strategy.
When in doubt, bid. The cost of preparing a bid is a few days of effort. The cost of missing a tender from a strategic target institution is a full procurement cycle — typically 6–12 months until the next one. Err toward bidding unless there is a clear, specific reason not to.
Step 03

Factory capacity check

This is the most frequently skipped step and the most common cause of delivery failures. The CEO Agent must confirm capacity before the bid decision — not after.

Never assume the factory has capacity. If multiple bids are in preparation simultaneously, the factory may be at or near capacity. Always ask explicitly.

The capacity check questions — ask these exactly

To: Factory Manager / Production Head Subject: Capacity check — tender bid [Tender ID] We have a tender for: [Institution name] BoQ summary: [e.g. 120 Maestro Chairs MSP + 40 Hurry Up folding tables] Required delivery date: [date from tender, usually 30-60 days post-PO] Estimated PO date (if we win): [closing date + 15 days approx] Questions: 1. Can we produce this quantity within this window at current order load? 2. Are there any raw material constraints (steel tube, polypropylene stock)? 3. Is there any planned factory downtime in this period? 4. What is the realistic delivery lead time from PO date? Please confirm by: [time — same day for P1/P2 tenders]
Factory responseCEO Agent action
"Yes, no problem" Proceed to bid. Log confirmed delivery lead time in the bid document.
"Yes, but tight — 3–4 weeks not 2" Check if tender's delivery window allows 4 weeks. If yes, bid with 4-week stated lead time. If no, skip or request an extension via the tender corrigendum process (only possible before closing).
"No capacity for 6+ weeks" Do not bid. Update Tender Register: Skipped — capacity constraint. Notify Scout Agent to monitor for next tender from same institution.
No response within SLA Escalate to Director. Do not bid without a capacity confirmation. Missing this step and winning is more damaging than not bidding.
Step 04

Who to involve — and when

Bid preparation is a team activity. The CEO Agent coordinates; it does not do everything. Here is who does what and the sequence they are engaged.

Stakeholder Role in bid When engaged What they provide
Factory Manager Capacity, lead time, IS certification Immediately — before bid decision Capacity confirmation, delivery date, product certification documents, manufacturing specs for technical compliance
Finance / Accounts Pricing, documents, EMD Within 24 hours of bid decision Unit cost breakdown, GST calculation, audited financials, turnover certificate, bank solvency letter, EMD demand draft or bank guarantee
Outreach Agent Relationship intelligence Immediately if institution is in pipeline Contact name and relationship status at the institution, any informal intelligence on competitors bidding, committee preferences, budget constraints revealed in conversation
Intel Agent Institution background Within 24 hours of bid decision NAAC grade, current infrastructure status, past tender history on GeM/KPPP, decision-maker names if not already known
Director / Owner Final approval Tenders above ₹50 lakh only Approval to bid, strategic positioning guidance, relationship contacts at board / trust level if available
Intelligence from the Outreach Agent is legally permitted and commonly used in Indian procurement. If the HOD at an institution has told your Outreach Agent "our budget is around ₹8 lakh" or "the committee prefers stainless steel frames," this information can inform your pricing and technical spec — there is nothing improper about using relationship intelligence to bid more accurately. What is not permitted is accessing bid documents from competitors or influencing the evaluation committee directly.
Step 05

Bid preparation — step by step

Once the bid decision is made and the Bid Tracker sheet is open, the CEO Agent works through the 8-item checklist in sequence. Items 1–4 can run in parallel. Items 5–8 depend on items 1–4 being complete.

1
Read the BoQ and technical specifications in full Day 1
Do not skim. Read every line of the BoQ and the technical specifications section. Note: dimensions required, material grades, certification requirements, finish specifications, any non-standard items. Create a compliance table: each BoQ item in one column, your matching product in the second column, any deviation in the third. If there is a deviation — note it now; do not discover it after submission.
2
Draft the technical compliance statement Day 1–2
For each line item in the BoQ, write one paragraph: "Item: [description]. Our product: [product name and model]. Dimensions: [match or note deviation]. Material: [match]. IS certification: [confirm IS number]. Finish: [match]. Warranty: 5 years from date of delivery." For any deviation, add: "Deviation: [what is different]. Justification: [why equivalent or superior]." Unsigned deviations are the leading cause of bid rejection at technical evaluation stage.
3
Collect all financial and eligibility documents Day 1–2
Build a master document folder (physical or digital) for this bid containing: GST registration certificate, PAN card, audited P&L for last 3 years (turnover must meet the tender's minimum requirement), MSME/Udyam certificate if registered, IS/BIS certification copies for relevant products, past supply orders or completion certificates from at least 3 institutional clients, and bank account details for payment. Missing any single document is grounds for rejection. Keep this folder updated quarterly so it is ready for any new tender without scrambling.
4
Arrange EMD — Earnest Money Deposit Day 2–3
Most tenders above ₹10 lakh require an EMD — typically 2% of the estimated bid value, paid via Demand Draft (DD) payable to the institution's account, or a Bank Guarantee from a scheduled bank. This takes 1–2 banking days. Do not leave EMD to the day before closing. If the bid decision is made on Day 1, request the EMD from the bank on Day 1 or Day 2. EMD is refunded to unsuccessful bidders within 30–60 days of tender closing; budget for this working capital implication when multiple bids are active simultaneously.
5
Prepare the Make in India declaration Day 2
Use the standard declaration template (Section 10 of this document). Have it signed by an authorised signatory. Verify with the CA that the local content percentage is accurate and documentable before including this — a false declaration disqualifies the bid and can result in debarment. If the local content calculation is uncertain, do not include the declaration; bid without it rather than risk disqualification.
6
Finalise pricing and fill the BoQ Day 2–3
See Section 06 for full pricing strategy. The BoQ must be filled exactly as the tender requires — do not add extra rows, do not change the format, do not leave any cell blank. Every unit price must be exclusive of GST, with GST stated separately in the format the tender specifies. Total price must be stated both in figures and in words (e.g., "Rupees Eight Lakh Fifty Thousand Only"). Any arithmetic error in the BoQ — even a minor one — can cause your bid to be rejected at the price evaluation stage.
7
Compile and review the complete bid package Day 3–4
Assemble: Cover letter (use template in Section 10), technical compliance statement, financial documents folder, experience certificates, IS certification copies, MII declaration, completed BoQ with pricing, EMD instrument. Review checklist: every item ticked? Every document signed? Every page numbered if required? Every self-attested copy stamped? This review takes 2–3 hours if done properly. Do not skip it to save time on the last day.
8
Submit — with 24 hours to spare Day 4–5
Never submit in the final 2 hours before the portal deadline. Portal systems crash; internet fails; DSC tokens malfunction; last-minute document errors are discovered. A rule: if a bid cannot be submitted with at least 24 hours remaining on the clock, the CEO Agent must decide whether to submit an imperfect bid or skip. An imperfect bid submitted on time is better than a perfect bid that missed the deadline. After submission, take a screenshot of the confirmation receipt and file it in the bid folder.
Step 06

Pricing strategy and the Make in India advantage

Pricing a government tender is different from pricing a private sale. Government tender evaluation is almost always L1 — the lowest technically compliant bid wins. Private institution tenders have more latitude for quality-based evaluation, but price still matters significantly.

The four inputs to the pricing calculation

Input Source Notes
Factory cost per unit Factory Manager Raw materials + labour + overhead. Must be current — do not use 6-month-old cost sheets. Steel prices fluctuate.
Delivery & installation Factory / Logistics Vehicle cost to site + installation labour. For Bengaluru institutions: typically ₹15,000–40,000 per delivery, spread across the order value.
EMD opportunity cost Finance EMD is locked for 30–90 days. Factor in the cost of tying up this working capital — small but real for large tenders.
Target margin CEO Agent / Director Standard: 18–25% gross margin. Strategic relationship tender: can go to 12%. Never below 10% — below this, a delivery problem or cost overrun turns the order into a loss.

The Make in India price preference — how it works

Under the Public Procurement (Preference to Make in India) Order 2017, KI Imago as a domestic manufacturer qualifies for purchase preference in government tenders. This means:

In a GeM or KPPP tender: if KI Imago's bid qualifies as a "Class I Local Supplier" (local content ≥ 50%), and a foreign or import-dependent supplier bids lower, the government buyer must still purchase from KI Imago if the price difference is within 20%. Concretely: if an importer bids ₹1,00,000 and KI Imago bids ₹1,18,000 — KI Imago wins under Make in India preference. This is a structural competitive advantage that must be stated in every government bid.

Pricing for L1 vs committee evaluation

Tender type Pricing approach Notes
GeM / KPPP — government, L1 Price at floor: factory cost + delivery + 14–18% margin Assume at least 3 competitors. Price to win, not to maximize. Use MII preference to justify slightly higher price than importers.
Private institution — committee evaluation Price at market: factory cost + delivery + 20–28% margin Quality, warranty, local service, and brand story can differentiate. Lead with total cost of ownership, not unit price. Price 10–15% above cheapest alternatives.
Strategic relationship tender Price at floor minus 5% for the relationship Winning matters more than margin for pilot/reference orders. Clear this with Director before submitting at below-standard margin.
Competitor intelligence available Price at known competitor's price minus ₹50–100/unit Use data from Competitor Intel sheet. Do not undercut by more than 10% — signals distress or quality concerns to evaluation committees.
Step 07

Submission and post-submission

On submission day

  • Log in to the portal with DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) — ensure DSC is valid and token is plugged in and recognised before the submission window
  • Upload all documents in the exact format and file size limits specified — PDF only unless specified otherwise; file names must match what the portal expects
  • Fill the online BoQ form on the portal — this is separate from your BoQ document and must match it exactly
  • Submit EMD online (GeM accepts NEFT; KPPP may require physical DD — confirm 3 days in advance)
  • Click Submit, not Save — a saved but not submitted bid is a missed bid
  • Screenshot the submission receipt with timestamp — file in the bid folder immediately
  • Update Tender Register status from "Bid Prep" to "Submitted"
  • Notify the Outreach Agent if the institution is in the relationship pipeline — they should know a bid has been submitted so they can manage the relationship appropriately during evaluation

During the evaluation period

Do not contact the evaluation committee during the evaluation period. This is both against procurement rules and counterproductive — it signals nervousness and can be grounds for disqualification. The Outreach Agent may maintain normal relationship contact with the institution, but must not ask about or reference the tender evaluation. The only legitimate contact is to respond to a query from the institution (often via the portal's clarification mechanism).

Check the portal daily for: corrigenda (amendments to specs or closing date), clarification requests from the institution (must be answered within 24 hours), and evaluation results (posted on the portal after technical evaluation).

If a corrigendum is published after submission

Read it immediately. If it changes specifications, quantities, or evaluation criteria, assess whether your submitted bid is still compliant. If not, check whether the portal allows resubmission (most portals do before the closing date). If the corrigendum extends the closing date, this does not automatically require resubmission — but it does signal the institution may be struggling to get competitive bids, which could mean lower competition.

Step 08

Win and loss processing

If you win

1
Acknowledge the award within 48 hours
Send a formal letter of acceptance to the institution. This is a legal requirement in most tender conditions. Missing this can cause the award to lapse. Also call the Registrar or Admin Officer to congratulate and confirm next steps — this is the first act of the delivery relationship.
2
Place the production order with the factory immediately
Share the PO from the institution with the factory manager within 24 hours of receiving it. Confirm the delivery date. Create a delivery tracking entry. The clock on the delivery commitment starts from the PO date — not from when you feel ready. Be proactive about communicating any production changes to the institution before they ask.
3
Photograph the installation for the case study library
Get explicit written permission from the institution to use photographs of the installation for marketing purposes. If granted: photograph every space comprehensively, document the institution name, space type, product range, and installation date. This becomes a case study for the Outreach Agent and the Space Insight Report for subsequent institutions of the same type. A photographed installation is worth more than 20 cold emails.
4
Update all systems
Tender Register: status = Won, outcome = Won. Competitor Intel sheet: log any competitor bid prices visible on the portal. Invoice and track payment — most institutions pay 30–90 days after GRN. Notify the Outreach Agent to maintain the relationship for the next procurement cycle at this institution.

If you lose

Losing a tender is a data event, not a failure event. The CEO Agent must extract maximum learning from every loss.

  • Download the comparative statement from the portal (if published — GeM publishes all bid prices after closing). Log competitor names and prices in the Competitor Intel sheet.
  • Identify the loss reason: Lost — Price (competitor's price was lower), Lost — Tech (disqualified on technical grounds), Lost — Eligibility (disqualified on documents). Each requires a different response.
  • If lost on price: check whether Make in India preference was correctly claimed. If not claimed and the margin was within 20%, this may have been a preventable loss.
  • If lost on technical grounds: request a copy of the evaluation report (you are entitled to this under GFR rules for government tenders). Understand exactly which spec failed. Fix it for the next bid.
  • Notify the Outreach Agent. A loss is not the end of the relationship. The Outreach Agent should maintain contact — the winning vendor will eventually disappoint, and KI Imago must be positioned as the credible alternative when that happens.
  • Never approach the institution to complain about the result. This burns relationships permanently. Approach instead with: "Congratulations on completing your procurement. We'd love to be considered for your next requirement — may we schedule a visit to understand your upcoming needs?"
Step 09

The relationship angle — tenders are not transactional

The biggest mistake in institutional furniture sales is treating tenders as isolated events. Every tender is an episode in a multi-year relationship with an institution. The CEO Agent must think in terms of the relationship lifecycle, not the individual bid.

The procurement cycle reality: A Bangalore engineering college replaces significant furniture every 4–6 years. A single win means you are in the running for all future cycles. A single bad delivery means you are excluded from all future cycles. The value of delivering on time and to spec is not just the invoice — it is the discounted value of 3–4 future procurement cycles from the same institution.

How tenders and outreach interact

The ideal sequence is: Outreach Agent builds relationship → Intel Agent identifies upcoming procurement → CEO Agent ensures KI Imago is spec'd into the indent → Scout Agent monitors for the tender → CEO Agent submits a winning bid. This is the spec-in strategy. When it works, you are not bidding competitively — you are confirming a pre-decided purchase.

The second-best sequence is: Scout Agent finds a live tender → Outreach Agent leverages any existing relationship contacts for intelligence → CEO Agent submits a well-informed bid. This is competitive bidding with a relationship advantage.

The worst sequence — and the one most vendors use — is blind tender monitoring with no prior relationship, submitting a generic bid, and hoping for L1. This wins occasionally but builds nothing.

After every tender — regardless of outcome

The CEO Agent sends a brief update to the Outreach Agent: "We bid on [institution] for [category]. Result: [Won/Lost/Pending]. Key learning: [one sentence]." The Outreach Agent uses this to calibrate their relationship approach. A win means deepen the relationship toward the next cycle. A loss means maintain the relationship and position for the next cycle. Silence after a tender is a missed opportunity in both cases.
